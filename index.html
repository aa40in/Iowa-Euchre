📖 Rules Explained
Goal

You and the player across from you are a team against the other two. Each hand you score one point per trick you win; the first team to 21 points wins.

Key point for newcomers

Once a suit is named as trump, its two Jacks become the highest cards in the game — above even the Aces. It's the one rule that trips people up, so keep an eye on your Jacks (details below).

Cards & deal

A 24-card deck (9, 10, J, Q, K, A in each suit ♠ ♥ ♦ ♣). Everyone gets 6 cards, using the whole deck — no draw pile.

Tricks

Play goes clockwise, one card each. That's a trick, and the highest card played wins it and leads the next one. There are 6 tricks per hand — each one your team wins is a point.

Following suit

You must play the led suit (the first card's suit) if you have it. If you don't, you may play anything — including a trump.

Trump

The bidder names one suit as trump. Any trump beats any non-trump — even the 9 of trump beats a non-trump Ace — and a higher trump beats a lower one. This matters most when a player is out of the led suit and plays a trump to steal the trick.

Card ranking (important)

In a normal suit: A, K, Q, J, 10, 9 (high to low).
In the trump suit, the two Jacks jump to the top:
• Highest: the Jack of the trump suit ("right bower").
• 2nd: the other same-color Jack, which also acts as trump ("left bower"). If ♥ is trump, the J♦ becomes a Heart.
• Then A, K, Q, 10, 9 of trump.
Example (♥ trump): J♥, J♦, A♥, K♥, Q♥, 10♥, 9♥.

Bidding

Each player, once, either passes or bids — a promise to win a number of tricks (1–6) in a trump suit of their choice. Each bid must beat the previous one. The highest bidder names trump, and their team must win at least that many tricks. The player to the winning bidder's left leads the first trick.

"Aces" (No Trump)

A bidder can choose Aces instead of a trump suit: no suit is trump, Jacks are ordinary, and the highest card of the led suit wins each trick.

Partner signals (1NT / 2NT)

Optional bids that describe your hand to your partner:
• 1NT — you hold one black Jack (♠/♣) and one red Jack (♥/♦).
• 2NT — a red Jack, a black Jack, and an Ace.
If your partner signals and nobody outbids it, you're expected to answer with a real bid.

Scoring

If the bidding team makes its promise, both teams score a point per trick won (e.g. a 4–2 hand scores +4 / +2). If the bidding team comes up short ("set"), it loses points equal to its bid while the other team scores its tricks as usual. If both teams hit 21 the same hand, the bidding team wins the tie.
