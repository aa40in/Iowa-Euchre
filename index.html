📖 Rules Explained
The Deal & Goal

Iowa Euchre is played with 24 cards (9 through Ace). Each player is dealt exactly 6 cards. The goal is to win tricks, playing to a maximum of 6 tricks per hand.

Bidding Process

Players take turns bidding the number of tricks they think they can win (1 to 6) along with their selected Trump suit or Aces (No Trump). If a Trump suit is selected, the Jack of that suit is the highest card (the "right bower"). The Jack of the same-color suit is treated as if it's also in the Trump suit as the second highest card in that suit (the "left bower"). The Trump suit cards, highest to lowest, are: right bower, left bower, A, K, Q, 10, 9.

Aces (No Trump) Mode

If "Aces" is chosen as the contract, Jacks behave as normal cards (9, 10, Jack, Queen, King, Ace). There are no bower swaps, suits remain natural, and Aces are high.

First Lead Rule

Play always starts with the player situated immediately to the left of the winning bidder, regardless of who dealt.

NT Conventions

To communicate with your partner without bidding high right away:
• 1NT Bid signals you hold at least one Jack and one Ace.
• 2NT Bid signals you hold at least one Red Jack, one Black Jack, and an Ace.

Scoring & Penalties

If your team meets the contract, you score trick points. If your team is "Set" (fails to meet the contract), you suffer a penalty equal to your contract bid (-bid points). The first team to 21 points wins!
