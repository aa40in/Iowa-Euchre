📖 Learn to Play: Euchre Rules
🃏 1. The Core Objective

Euchre is a cooperative trick-taking card game played with a 24-card deck (9, 10, Jack, Queen, King, and Ace of each suit). You play in a partnership with the AI teammate situated across from you to out-score the opponents.

📣 2. The Bidding Phase

Before standard play, players take turns bidding. A bid states how many tricks (from 1 to 6) your partnership promises to win during the hand, along with a chosen Trump Suit (which beats all normal cards) or "Aces" (No Trump).

⚠️ Rule: You cannot bid a 1-trick contract unless everyone has passed before you, making early bids highly competitive!

🔄 3. Card Rankings & Bowers
  • Under standard Trump suits: Jacks change suits. The Jack of your chosen trump is the Right Bower (highest card). The Jack of the *same color* suit becomes a trump card and is the Left Bower (second highest card). For example, if Hearts are trump, the J♥ is top, and the J♦ is second!
  • Under "Aces" (No Trump) Mode: No cards change suits and there are no Bower cards. Ranks follow strict face value: Aces are high, Jacks are normal cards between 10 and Queen.
⚔️ 4. Trick-Taking Play

The player directly to the left of the winning bidder leads the first card. Each player must play a card matching the suit led if possible (Following Suit). If you have no cards of that suit, you can play a Trump card to cut in, or discard an off-suit card (sluff).

💡 Pro-Tip: The Left Bower belongs fully to the Trump suit. If Spades are trump, playing the Jack of Clubs is considered a Spade card!

📈 5. Scoring & Winning
  • If your team wins at least as many tricks as you bid, your team is successful. Both teams score 1 point per trick they won.
  • If your team fails to make the bid, your team is Set, suffering a -5 point penalty, while the defenders get 1 point per trick they won.
  • First team to reach 21 points wins the game!
